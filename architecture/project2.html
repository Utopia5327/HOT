The Pangolin 1:1| Terrametric Fabrication+ Workshop, a collaboration between Novatr and Ant Studio, was an 8-day event from 21st-28th December 2019 at the School of Planning and Architecture, New Delhi. It focused on blending sustainability with computational design and included a challenge to learn, explore, and construct a pavilion on campus.

about.

As the name of the workshop suggests, it aimed to explore sustainability and computation design The word Terrametric coined by Novatr comes from the words "Terra" and "Parametric". Terra refers to the earth and land whereas parametric refers to the process of algorithmic thinking in design.

The pavilion, conceived as a tribute to the pangolin, featured terracotta tiles shaped to resemble the animal's scales. These tiles were mounted onto curved metal beams, with precise angle adjustments to achieve the pavilion's distinctive form. The design was developed using Grasshopper and Rhino, with the terracotta tiles sponsored by a local brand and fabricated through water-jet cutting. The build involved 380 individual tiles, rolled mild steel frames that were bent and assembled on-site, 72 hours of intensive fabrication work, and participation from 25 students from across the country.
